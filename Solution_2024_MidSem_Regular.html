Q1. a) Five-Number Summary
[3 Marks]
Consider waiting times (in minutes): 4.21, 5.55, 3.02, 5.13, 4.77, 2.34, 3.54, 3.20, 4.50, 6.10, 0.38, 5.12, 6.46, 6.19, 3.79. Determine the five-number summary: Minimum, Q1, Median, Q3, Maximum. Based on the results obtained, give an interpretation about the data.

🎓 Theory & Concepts

The Five-Number Summary provides a quick overview of a dataset's distribution. It consists of the Minimum, 1st Quartile (Q1), Median (Q2), 3rd Quartile (Q3), and Maximum.

To find these, the data MUST be sorted in ascending order first.

Position of Median = (n+1)/2
Position of Q1 = (n+1)/4
Position of Q3 = 3(n+1)/4

If the mean > median or if the right tail (Max - Median) is much longer than the left tail (Median - Min), the data is positively skewed (skewed to the right).

⚙️ Step-by-Step Algorithm

1
Sort all values from smallest to largest.
2
Identify Min and Max (the first and last values).
3
Find Median (the exact middle value for odd n, or average of two middle values for even n).
4
Find Q1 and Q3 (the medians of the lower half and upper half of the data respectively).

📝 Full Exam Solution

Step 1: Sort the Data (n = 15)
0.38, 2.34, 3.02, 3.20, 3.54, 3.79, 4.21, 4.50, 4.77, 5.12, 5.13, 5.55, 6.10, 6.19, 6.46
Step 2: Find the Five Numbers
  • Minimum: 0.38
  • Median (Position 8): 4.50
  • Q1 (Median of first 7 values): 4th value = 3.20
  • Q3 (Median of last 7 values): 12th value = 5.55
  • Maximum: 6.46
Step 3: Interpretation
The data is slightly positively skewed. We can observe that the right tail (Max - Median = 6.46 - 4.50 = 1.96) is slightly longer than the left tail (Median - Min = 4.50 - 0.38 = 4.12). Wait, let's re-examine. Actually, left tail (4.12) is much longer than right tail (1.96). Let's check Q1-Min (2.82) vs Max-Q3 (0.91). This implies negative skewness. Let's calculate the mean: sum = 64.3, mean = 64.3/15 = 4.286. Since Mean (4.286) < Median (4.50), the data is actually negatively skewed (left skewed).
Five-number summary: (0.38, 3.20, 4.50, 5.55, 6.46). Data is negatively skewed.

Q2. Conditional Probability
[5 Marks]
A study revealed that 35% of employees admit to keeping quiet when they see co-worker misconduct. Suppose 75% of employees who admit to keeping quiet when they see co-worker misconduct call in sick when they are well. In addition, suppose that 40% of the employees who call in sick when they are well admit to keeping quiet when they see co-worker misconduct. Find: (a) P(calls in sick AND keeps quiet), (b) P(keeps quiet OR calls in sick), (c) P(does not keep quiet | calls in sick), (d) P(neither), (e) P(keeps quiet AND does not call in sick).

🎓 Theory & Concepts

This is a classic conditional probability problem. Let's define the events clearly:

  • Q = Employee keeps quiet.
  • S = Employee calls in sick when well.
P(A ∩ B) = P(A | B) × P(B)
P(A ∪ B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A ∩ B)
P(A' | B) = 1 - P(A | B)

📝 Full Exam Solution

Given Probabilities:
P(Q) = 0.35
P(S | Q) = 0.75
P(Q | S) = 0.40
Preliminary Calculations:
Intersection: P(Q ∩ S) = P(S | Q) × P(Q) = 0.75 × 0.35 = 0.2625
Probability of S: P(S) = P(Q ∩ S) / P(Q | S) = 0.2625 / 0.40 = 0.65625
a) Calls in sick and admits to keeping quiet:
P(Q ∩ S) = 0.2625
b) Admits to keeping quiet or calls in sick:
P(Q ∪ S) = P(Q) + P(S) - P(Q ∩ S) = 0.35 + 0.65625 - 0.2625 = 0.74375
c) Given calls in sick, does not keep quiet:
P(Q' | S) = 1 - P(Q | S) = 1 - 0.40 = 0.60
d) Neither keeps quiet nor calls in sick:
P(Q' ∩ S') = 1 - P(Q ∪ S) = 1 - 0.74375 = 0.25625
e) Admits to keeping quiet and does not call in sick:
P(Q ∩ S') = P(Q) - P(Q ∩ S) = 0.35 - 0.2625 = 0.0875
Q3. a) Poisson Distribution
[3 Marks]
Flaws per foot in rolls follow a Poisson distribution with a mean of 0.2 flaw per foot. What is the probability that in a:
i. 1-foot roll, there will be at least 2 flaws?
ii. 50-foot roll, there will be greater than or equal to 5 flaws and less than or equal to 8 flaws?

🎓 Theory & Concepts

The Poisson distribution models the number of events occurring in a fixed interval of time or space. The key parameter is \(\lambda\) (lambda), which is the average rate of occurrence.

Crucial rule: If the interval size changes, \(\lambda\) must be scaled proportionally!

\[ P(X = k) = \frac{e^{-\lambda} \lambda^k}{k!} \]

📝 Full Exam Solution

Part (i): 1-foot roll
For a 1-foot roll, \(\lambda = 0.2\).
We want P(X ≥ 2). It's easier to use the complement rule: P(X ≥ 2) = 1 - P(X < 2) = 1 - [P(X = 0) + P(X = 1)]
\[ P(X = 0) = \frac{e^{-0.2}(0.2)^0}{0!} = e^{-0.2} = 0.8187 \]
\[ P(X = 1) = \frac{e^{-0.2}(0.2)^1}{1!} = 0.8187 \times 0.2 = 0.1637 \]
P(X < 2) = 0.8187 + 0.1637 = 0.9824
P(X ≥ 2) = 1 - 0.9824 = 0.0176 (or 0.0175 without intermediate rounding)
Part (ii): 50-foot roll
For a 50-foot roll, the new mean is \(\lambda = 50 \times 0.2 = 10\).
We want P(5 ≤ X ≤ 8) = P(X=5) + P(X=6) + P(X=7) + P(X=8).
\[ P(X = 5) = \frac{e^{-10}(10)^5}{5!} = \frac{0.0000454 \times 100000}{120} = 0.0378 \]
\[ P(X = 6) = \frac{e^{-10}(10)^6}{6!} = 0.0631 \]
\[ P(X = 7) = \frac{e^{-10}(10)^7}{7!} = 0.0901 \]
\[ P(X = 8) = \frac{e^{-10}(10)^8}{8!} = 0.1126 \]
Total = 0.0378 + 0.0631 + 0.0901 + 0.1126 = 0.3036
Q3. b) Total Probability
[2 Marks]
An individual has 3 different email accounts. Most of her messages, in fact 70%, come into account #1, whereas 20% come into account #2 and the remaining 10% into account #3. Of the messages into account #1, only 1% are spam, whereas the corresponding percentages for accounts #2 and #3 are 2% and 5%, respectively. What is the probability that a randomly selected message is spam?

dYZ" Theory & Concepts

This problem uses the Law of Total Probability. If an event can occur through several mutually exclusive and exhaustive paths (the different email accounts), the total probability is the sum of the probabilities of each path.

\[ P(S) = \sum P(S | A_i) \cdot P(A_i) \]

dY"? Full Exam Solution

Given:
P(Account 1) = \( P(A_1) = 0.70 \), \( P(\text{Spam} | A_1) = 0.01 \)
P(Account 2) = \( P(A_2) = 0.20 \), \( P(\text{Spam} | A_2) = 0.02 \)
P(Account 3) = \( P(A_3) = 0.10 \), \( P(\text{Spam} | A_3) = 0.05 \)
Applying Total Probability:
\[ P(\text{Spam}) = P(S|A_1)P(A_1) + P(S|A_2)P(A_2) + P(S|A_3)P(A_3) \]
\[ P(\text{Spam}) = (0.01 \times 0.70) + (0.02 \times 0.20) + (0.05 \times 0.10) \]
\[ P(\text{Spam}) = 0.007 + 0.004 + 0.005 = 0.016 \]
P(Spam) = 0.016 (or 1.6%)
Q4. a) Joint Probability Mass Function
[3 Marks]
The discrete random variables X and Y have joint probability mass function. Find value of constant k and marginal probability functions for X and Y.

dYZ" Theory & Concepts

Note: The specific PMF table/equation was missing from the original exam text, so here is the general approach to solve it.

A Joint PMF \( P(X=x, Y=y) \) must satisfy two conditions:
1. All probabilities are non-negative.
2. The sum of all probabilities over all possible combinations of (x, y) must equal 1.

dY"? Full Exam Solution

Step 1: Finding the constant \( k \)
Set up the equation: \( \sum_x \sum_y P(X=x, Y=y) = 1 \). Substitute the given probabilities involving \( k \) into the sum, and solve for \( k \).
Step 2: Finding Marginal PMFs
To find the marginal probability for \( X \), sum over all \( Y \):
\[ P_X(x) = \sum_y P(X=x, Y=y) \]
To find the marginal probability for \( Y \), sum over all \( X \):
\[ P_Y(y) = \sum_x P(X=x, Y=y) \]
Q4. b) Hypergeometric Distribution
[2 Marks]
From a lot of 10 items containing 3 defective items, a sample of 4 items is drawn at random without replacement. Let the random variable X denote the number of defective items in the sample.
i) Find the probability distribution of X
ii) Find P(X ≥ 1)
iii) Find E(X)

dYZ" Theory & Concepts

Since we are drawing without replacement, this follows a Hypergeometric Distribution.

\[ P(X=x) = \frac{\binom{K}{x} \binom{N-K}{n-x}}{\binom{N}{n}} \]

Where:
\( N = 10 \) (total items)
\( K = 3 \) (defective items)
\( N-K = 7 \) (good items)
\( n = 4 \) (sample size)

dY"? Full Exam Solution

i) Probability Distribution of X:
Total possible ways to draw 4 items from 10: \( \binom{10}{4} = 210 \).
\( x \) Numerator: \( \binom{3}{x} \binom{7}{4-x} \) \( P(X=x) \)
0 \( \binom{3}{0}\binom{7}{4} = 1 \times 35 = 35 \) \( \frac{35}{210} = \frac{1}{6} \)
1 \( \binom{3}{1}\binom{7}{3} = 3 \times 35 = 105 \) \( \frac{105}{210} = \frac{1}{2} \)
2 \( \binom{3}{2}\binom{7}{2} = 3 \times 21 = 63 \) \( \frac{63}{210} = \frac{3}{10} \)
3 \( \binom{3}{3}\binom{7}{1} = 1 \times 7 = 7 \) \( \frac{7}{210} = \frac{1}{30} \)
ii) Find P(X ≥ 1):
Using the complement rule: \( P(X \ge 1) = 1 - P(X = 0) \)
\[ P(X \ge 1) = 1 - \frac{1}{6} = \frac{5}{6} \approx 0.8333 \]
iii) Expected Value E(X):
\( x \) \( P(X=x) \) \( x \cdot P(X=x) \)
0 \( 1/6 \) 0
1 \( 1/2 \) 0.5
2 \( 3/10 \) 0.6
3 \( 1/30 \) 0.1
E(X) = Sum 1.2
\[ E(X) = \sum x \cdot P(X=x) = 1.2 \] Note: For a Hypergeometric Distribution, \( E(X) = n \cdot \frac{K}{N} = 4 \cdot \frac{3}{10} = 1.2 \). This matches perfectly!
Q5. Naive Bayes Classification
[5 Marks]
If the weather is Snowy, then the player will play or not? Use Naive Bayes.
(Based on a dataset of 14 days, where "Player Play = Yes" occurs 7 times, and "Player Play = No" occurs 7 times. "Snowy" occurs 1 time when Play=Yes, and 3 times when Play=No).

dYZ" Theory & Concepts

Naive Bayes is a probabilistic classifier based on applying Bayes' theorem with strong (naive) independence assumptions between the features.

\[ P(\text{Class} | \text{Feature}) = \frac{P(\text{Feature} | \text{Class}) \cdot P(\text{Class})}{P(\text{Feature})} \]

dY"? Full Exam Solution

Step 1: Prior Probabilities
Total rows = 14
\( P(\text{Play=Yes}) = \frac{7}{14} = 0.5 \)
\( P(\text{Play=No}) = \frac{7}{14} = 0.5 \)
Step 2: Conditional Probabilities for Snowy
\( P(\text{Snowy} | \text{Yes}) = \frac{1}{7} \)
\( P(\text{Snowy} | \text{No}) = \frac{3}{7} \)
Step 3: Total Probability of Snowy
\( P(\text{Snowy}) = P(\text{Snowy} | \text{Yes})P(\text{Yes}) + P(\text{Snowy} | \text{No})P(\text{No}) \)
\( P(\text{Snowy}) = (\frac{1}{7} \times 0.5) + (\frac{3}{7} \times 0.5) = \frac{4}{14} = \frac{2}{7} \)
Step 4: Apply Bayes' Theorem
\( P(\text{Yes} | \text{Snowy}) = \frac{P(\text{Snowy} | \text{Yes}) \cdot P(\text{Yes})}{P(\text{Snowy})} \)
\( P(\text{Yes} | \text{Snowy}) = \frac{\frac{1}{7} \times \frac{1}{2}}{\frac{2}{7}} = \frac{\frac{1}{14}}{\frac{4}{14}} = \frac{1}{4} = 0.25 \)
The probability that the player will play when the weather is snowy is 0.25 (25%).
Q6. a) Bayes' Theorem (Medical Test)
[3 Marks]
Incidence of a rare disease: Only 1 in 1000 adults is afflicted. When an individual actually has the disease, a positive result will occur 99% of the time. An individual without the disease will show a positive test result 2% of the time. If tested positive, what is the probability that the individual has the disease?

dY"? Full Exam Solution

Given:
Let \( D \) = Has Disease, \( + \) = Positive Test
\( P(D) = 0.001 \) (1 in 1000)
\( P(D') = 0.999 \)
\( P(+|D) = 0.99 \) (Sensitivity)
\( P(+|D') = 0.02 \) (False Positive Rate)
Total Probability of a Positive Test \( P(+) \):
\[ P(+) = P(+|D)P(D) + P(+|D')P(D') \]
\[ P(+) = (0.99 \times 0.001) + (0.02 \times 0.999) \]
\[ P(+) = 0.00099 + 0.01998 = 0.02097 \]
Bayes' Theorem \( P(D|+) \):
\[ P(D|+) = \frac{P(+|D)P(D)}{P(+)} \]
\[ P(D|+) = \frac{0.00099}{0.02097} \approx 0.0472 \]
Probability of having the disease = 4.72%
Q6. b) Normal Distribution
[2 Marks]
A certain machine makes electrical resistors having mean resistance of 40 ohms and standard deviation of 2 ohms. If the resistance follows a normal distribution:
i) What percentage of resistors have resistances that exceed 43 ohms?
ii) What percentage of resistors will have resistances that exceed 43 ohms to the nearest ohm?

dYZ" Theory & Concepts

To find normal distribution probabilities, we convert the value \( X \) to a standard \( Z \)-score.

\[ Z = \frac{X - \mu}{\sigma} \]

For values rounded "to the nearest ohm", we apply a continuity correction. "Exceeds 43 to the nearest ohm" means the true continuous value must be greater than 42.5.

dY"? Full Exam Solution

Given:
Mean \( \mu = 40 \)
Std Dev \( \sigma = 2 \)
i) Exceeds 43 ohms: \( P(X > 43) \)
\[ z = \frac{43 - 40}{2} = 1.5 \]
Using standard normal tables:
\[ P(Z > 1.5) = 1 - P(Z \le 1.5) = 1 - 0.9332 = 0.0668 \] Percentage = 6.68%
ii) Exceeds 43 ohms to the nearest ohm: \( P(X > 42.5) \)
\[ z = \frac{42.5 - 40}{2} = 1.25 \]
Using standard normal tables:
\[ P(Z > 1.25) = 1 - P(Z \le 1.25) = 1 - 0.8944 = 0.1056 \] Percentage = 10.56%
